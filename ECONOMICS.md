# Block-Cell Economics — The Full Loop

*The center produces. Technology grows it. Military burns it. The oscillation IS the economy.*

## Turn Order — Round Robin with UNO Disruption

- 4 players around a clock: Q1 → Q2 → Q3 → Q4 → Q1...
- **UNO cards**: draw 1 random card every time you capture a non-pawn piece
- Cards can also be BOUGHT with points (skip turn = extra resources as income)
- UNO effects: skip opponent, reverse turn order, draw-2 (opponent forced to draw cards instead of moving), wild (choose any effect)

## The Tick-Tack Oscillation

Players choose which of 2 ticks they're active on:
- **Tick A**: players 1+3 active (the two competing antis)
- **Tack B**: players 2+4 active (the other two competing antis)
- Blinker oscillation: tick-tack-tick-tack
- The information bouncing between ticks IS the Blue/Red channel

## The Central Mine — 3×3 Resource Production

The center 3×3 squares produce RAW MINERALS each turn.

### Production Rate (dynamic, based on board state):

| Board state | Production per tick | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Low activity (cooperation) | 4 minerals (stable baseline) | Peaceful = steady |
| High tech competition | 5-8 minerals (growth) | Innovation drives surplus |
| High destructive war | 1-3 minerals (scarcity) | War burns the fields |
| Post-war scarcity (<2) | Recovery mode | Both sides MUST cooperate or collapse |

### The Cycle:
```
Cooperation → surplus → one side wants the surplus → war
War → destruction → scarcity → both sides must cooperate → recovery
Recovery → cooperation → surplus → cycle repeats
```

This IS the Kondratieff wave. The economy oscillates between growth and destruction. The players can't stop it — they can only choose WHEN to fight and WHEN to trade.

## Military vs Technology Split

### Technology Side (Noble/Economic player):
- Produces GOODS from raw minerals (value-add)
- Goods gain value at technological nations
- Can establish TRADE ROUTES (automatic production + selling at fixed rate)
- Trade route price = f(costs, raw material availability)
- Skipping turns generates extra resources (investment)
- CANNOT fight directly — must hire military or buy mercenaries

### Military Side (General/War player):
- War generates NO income — only burns resources
- MUST buy resources from the tech side (no self-sufficiency)
- CAN conquer territory (expand control of center mine)
- CAN promote pieces (promote pawn to higher on command)
- CAN recruit new pawns from village (costs materials)
- CAN transition pieces without value loss (promote/demote freely)

### The Dependency:
```
Military needs Tech for: resources, equipment, new pieces
Tech needs Military for: protection, territory control, deterrence
Neither can survive alone.
```

## Piece Manufacturing

Pieces are MADE from materials. They are FINITE.

| Piece | Material cost | Upkeep per turn | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flint-Pawn | 1 mineral | 0 | Free upkeep, basic unit |
| Knight | 3 minerals | 1/turn | Mobile, moderate |
| Bishop | 3 minerals | 1/turn | Range, moderate |
| Rook | 5 minerals | 2/turn | Heavy, expensive to maintain |
| Queen | 9 minerals | 3/turn | Devastating but drains economy |
| Mercenary (any) | 2× base cost | 3/turn | Likely to defect if unpaid |

### Upkeep Failure:
- Can't pay upkeep → piece becomes IDLE (can't move for 1 turn)
- 2 consecutive unpaid turns → piece DEFECTS (joins highest bidder)
- 3 consecutive → piece LEAVES the board (returns half value)

## Mercenaries

- Buy any high-level piece at 2× normal cost
- They're skilled but EXPENSIVE (3/turn upkeep regardless of type)
- If you miss 1 payment: 50% chance they defect to opponent
- If you miss 2: they leave with half your stored resources
- Mercenaries have their own personality (Wrecker/Wall/Glitch)
- They're the most powerful pieces but the highest economic risk

## Trade Routes

Established between any two allied or neutral quadrants:
- **Automatic**: produces and sells goods each tick without player action
- **Fixed rate**: price set at creation, doesn't change with market
- **OR market rate**: price fluctuates with supply/demand (higher risk, higher reward)
- **Requires**: safe path on the board (no enemy pieces blocking the route)
- **Disruption**: enemy piece on the trade route = route broken until cleared

## The Resource Trap

```
Start: 4 minerals/tick (stable)
  ↓ tech grows production to 6
  ↓ military sees surplus, attacks
  ↓ war burns production to 2
  ↓ both sides running out of stock
  ↓ no resources = no upkeep = pieces defect/leave
  ↓ FORCED cooperation to recover production
  ↓ ceasefire, rebuild, back to 4
  ↓ tech grows again...
```

**The trap**: you CAN'T fight to 1 resource and have no stock. That's guaranteed collapse for BOTH sides. The game FORCES cooperation through scarcity, not through rules. The physics of the economy make war unsustainable long-term.

This is the conservation law from OoC-Mana applied to chess economics. Energy in = energy out. War doesn't create value, only redistributes and destroys it.

## Skip Turn Economics

Skipping your turn is NOT doing nothing. It's:
- +2 minerals income (passive investment)
- +1 UNO card draw (strategic reserve)
- Army upkeep still costs (you save on action but not maintenance)
- The OPTIMAL strategy sometimes IS doing nothing
- This is the "Luffy naps" mechanic — waiting for the world to reveal itself

## Win Condition Revisited

With economics, victory isn't just Nash:
- **Nash equilibrium** = all 4 players agree the economy is stable (GoL cell ALIVE)
- **Economic victory** = one side controls >60% of mineral production (dominant but fragile)
- **Collapse** = total minerals < 2 for 3 consecutive ticks (cell DIES, reset)
- **Trade victory** = all 4 quadrants connected by trade routes (rare, cooperative, best outcome)

The BEST outcome is a pulsing economy where war and peace alternate productively. The WORST is runaway war that collapses production. The game teaches: controlled conflict is healthy, uncontrolled conflict is fatal.
